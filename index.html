About Me

I'm a Wilmington, NC native pursuing a B.S. in Intelligent Systems Engineering at UNC Wilmington, concentrating in cyber-physical systems. I started taking college courses full-time at 16 through Isaac Bear Early College High School, completed over 50 credit hours early, and haven't looked back. I graduate in Spring 2027.

My most meaningful work has been with the Helping Hand Project, where I help design and build prosthetic devices for real clients. Last semester I helped fabricate components for a prosthetic hand for a young girl — and got to be there when she tried it on for the first time. That experience is the clearest example I have of why engineering matters.

Outside the lab I teach surfing to children with disabilities as a charity instructor, compete with the UNCW Surfing Club, and stay involved with the ASBPA. This summer I'll be studying abroad in Prague, participating in an entrepreneurship program to pitch an AI startup.

I'm actively looking for internships in robotics and intelligent systems. I bring curiosity, a systems-level mindset, and a track record of showing up for hard problems — in and out of the classroom.
